While many libraries offer overviews of their services through their Websites only a small number of health sciences libraries provide Web-based virtual tours. These tours typically feature photographs of major service areas along with textual descriptions. This article describes the process for planning producing and implementing a next-generation virtual tour in which a variety of media elements are integrated: photographic images 360-degree "virtual reality" views textual descriptions and contextual floor plans. Hardware and software tools used in the project are detailed along with a production timeline and budget tips for streamlining the process and techniques for improving production. This paper is intended as a starting guide for other libraries considering an investment in such a project. Production of the next-generation library virtual tour.
