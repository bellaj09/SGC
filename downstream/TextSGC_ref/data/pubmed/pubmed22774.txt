The purported affective impact of virtual reality VR and active video gaming AVG systems is a key marketing strategy underlying their use in stroke rehabilitation yet little is known as to how affective constructs are measured or linked to intervention outcomes. The purpose of this scoping review is to 1 explore how motivation enjoyment engagement immersion and presence are measured or described in VR/AVG interventions for patients with stroke; 2 identify directional relationships between these constructs; and 3 evaluate their impact on motor learning outcomes. What is the impact of user affect on motor learning in virtual environments after stroke? A scoping review.
