Several changes introduced in the management of trauma during the last two decades have considerably decreased the practical exposure to bleeding trauma patients by residents and young surgeons. Hemorrhage still represents the second cause of death from trauma worldwide and the surgical maneuvers required for its control must be learned and practised in specific courses. These courses address the "second hour" of trauma beyond ATLS and also emphasize the decision-making process communication among team members and discussion of clinical scenarios. The significant progress made in simulation technologies and virtual reality systems have yet to replace living tissue models to train surgeons in the rapid control of active bleeding although that replacement is probably not far away.
