The success rate for treating phobias with in vivo exposure is high. Unfortunately few phobics less than 15-20% ever seek treatment. Virtual reality VR exposure therapy is also proving to be highly effective. The present surveys assessed 162 students high in fear of spiders. In Study 1 when asked to choose between multisession in vivo exposure vs. multisession VR exposure therapy 81% chose VR. In Study 2 comparing one-session in vivo versus multisession VR exposure therapy 89% chose VR. Results suggest that VR exposure therapy may prove valuable for increasing the number of phobics who seek treatment. Redefining therapeutic success with virtual reality exposure therapy.
