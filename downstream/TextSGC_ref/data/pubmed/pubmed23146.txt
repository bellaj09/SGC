Virtual reality is used in marketing research to shape food selection and purchase decisions. Could it be used to counteract the marketing of less-nutritious foods and teach healthier food selection? This article presents interviews with Raymond Burke Ph.D. of Indiana University Bloomington and Rachel Jones M.P.H. of the University of Utah College of Health. Topics covered include new marketing research technologies including virtual reality simulations; retailing and shopper behavior; and the use of virtual grocery stores to help students explore quality of diet and food/nutrient relationships. The interviewees discuss how the technologies they have developed fit into research and behavior change related to obesity and diabetes. New directions in the use of virtual reality for food shopping: marketing and education perspectives.
