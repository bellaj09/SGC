The 2019 novel coronavirus COVID-19 pandemic has created significant challenges to the delivery of care for patients with advanced head and neck cancer requiring multimodality therapy. Performing major head and neck ablative surgery and reconstruction is a particular concern given the extended duration and aerosolizing nature of these cases. In this manuscript we describe our surgical approach to provide timely reconstructive care and minimize infectious risk to the providers patients and families. Major head and neck reconstruction during the COVID-19 pandemic: The University of Pittsburgh approach.
