People with chronic arm impairment should exercise intensely to regain their abilities but frequently lack motivation leading to poor rehabilitation outcome. One promising way to increase motivation is through interpersonal rehabilitation games which allow patients to compete or cooperate together with other people. However such games have mainly been evaluated with unimpaired subjects and little is known about how they affect motivation and exercise intensity in people with chronic arm impairment. Competitive and cooperative arm rehabilitation games played by a patient and unimpaired person: effects on motivation and exercise intensity.
