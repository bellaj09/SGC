Paralysis whether caused by spinal cord injury neurodegenerative disease or other factors poses a host of issues for patients. These include not just the inability to move parts of their bodies but potential problems with communication and bladder control as well. Fortunately the last decade has seen promising technology advances to address these concerns. Although most are still in the research stage these technologies-including brain-computer interfaces BCIs exoskeletons and robotics-may one day improve the lives of people with paralysis.
