Osteocytes are the most abundant bone cells. They are completely encased in mineralized tissue sitting inside lacunae that are connected by a multitude of canaliculi. In recent years the osteocyte network has been shown to fulfill endocrine functions and to communicate with a number of other organs. This review addresses emerging knowledge on the connectome of the lacunocanalicular network in different types of bone tissue. Towards a Connectomic Description of the Osteocyte Lacunocanalicular Network in Bone.
