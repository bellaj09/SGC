SARS-CoV-2 was first detected in December 2019 in the Chinese city of Wuhan and has since spread across the world. At present the virus has infected over 1.7 million people and caused over 100\u2009000 deaths worldwide. Research is currently focused on understanding the acute infection and developing effective treatment strategies. In view of the magnitude of the epidemic we conducted a speculative review of possible medium- and long-term neurological consequences of SARS-CoV-2 infection with particular emphasis on neurodegenerative and neuropsychiatric diseases of neuroinflammatory origin based on the available evidence on neurological symptoms of acute SARS-CoV-2 infection. Impact of SARS-CoV-2 infection on neurodegenerative and neuropsychiatric diseases: a delayed pandemic?
