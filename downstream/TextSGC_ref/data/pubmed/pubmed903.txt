In December 2019 an outbreak of the novel coronavirus the coronavirus disease 2019 COVID-19 probably occurred in Wuhan China. By March 2020 the World Health Organization WHO had declared a pandemic. Containment measures such as social distancing and hand hygiene were recommended. In this study we start from the hypothesis that engaging with containment measures in a pandemic situation should be more comfortable for some people than for other people. Thus individual differences should be associated with engagement with containment measures. Personality differences and COVID-19: are extroversion and conscientiousness personality traits associated with engagement with containment measures?
