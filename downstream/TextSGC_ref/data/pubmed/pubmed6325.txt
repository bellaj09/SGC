Resting-state functional MRI has been proposed as a new biomarker of prodromal neurodegenerative disorders but it has been poorly investigated in the idiopathic form of rapid-eye-movement sleep behavior disorder IRBD a clinical harbinger of subsequent synucleinopathy. Particularly a complex-network approach has not been tested to study brain functional connectivity in IRBD patients. Disruption of posterior brain functional connectivity and its relation to cognitive impairment in idiopathic REM sleep behavior disorder.
