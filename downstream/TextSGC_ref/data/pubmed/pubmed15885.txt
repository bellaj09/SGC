The Approaches and Study Skills Inventory for Students ASSIST has been used previously to assess the learning approaches among students in higher education but reports of its use with occupational therapy students are rare. This study investigated the factor structure of the ASSIST in a cross-cultural sample of undergraduate occupational therapy students and examined whether the factor structure from specific participant groups from different cross-cultural contexts was consistent. Factor analysis of the Approaches and Study Skills Inventory for Students in a cross-cultural occupational therapy undergraduate student sample.
