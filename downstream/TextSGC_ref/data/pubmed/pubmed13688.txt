A 42-year-old male was admitted with shortness of breath secondary to suspected heart failure and chest infection. An echocardiogram revealed a dilated and impaired left ventricle; ejection fraction 29% with a large mobile thrombus within the left ventricular apex. Due to the presence of liver dysfunction vitamin K antagonists were deemed inappropriate; thus the decision was taken to use the novel anticoagulation agent Apixaban.
