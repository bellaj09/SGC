Recent research identifies unhealthful weight-control behaviors fasting vomiting or laxative abuse induced by a negative experience of the body as the common antecedents of both obesity and eating disorders. In particular according to the allocentric lock hypothesis individuals with obesity may be locked to an allocentric observer view negative memory of the body that is no longer updated by contrasting egocentric representations driven by perception. In other words these patients may be locked to an allocentric negative representation of their body that their sensory inputs are no longer able to update even after a demanding diet and a significant weight loss. Virtual reality for enhancing the cognitive behavioral treatment of obesity with binge eating disorder: randomized controlled study with one-year follow-up.
