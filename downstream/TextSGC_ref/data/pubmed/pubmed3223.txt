Melancholic depression is a relatively homogenous subtype of major depressive disorders MDD. The condition has several endogenous symptoms and represents strong biological components. However its specific neurobiological mechanisms remain unknown. Previous neuroimaging findings indicated that default mode network DMN is closely related to MDD. The present study examined the network homogeneity NH of the DMN in patients with melancholic MDD. Aberrant default mode network homogeneity in patients with first-episode treatment-naive melancholic depression.
