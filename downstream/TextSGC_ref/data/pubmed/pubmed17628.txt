Recent high-profile cases have heightened the need for a formal structure to monitor achievement and maintenance of surgical competence. Logbooks morbidity and mortality meetings videos and direct observation of operations using a checklist motion analysis devices and virtual reality simulators are effective tools for teaching and evaluating surgical skills. As the operating theater is also a place for training there must be protocols and guidelines including mandatory standards for supervision to ensure that patient care is not compromised. Patients appreciate frank communication and honesty from surgeons regarding their expertise and level of competence. To ensure that surgical competence is maintained and keeps pace with technologic advances professional registration bodies have been promoting programs for recertification.
