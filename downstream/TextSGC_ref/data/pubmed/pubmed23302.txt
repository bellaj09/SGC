This review investigates the available literature that addresses the impact simulator training has on patient outcomes. The authors conducted a comprehensive literature search of studies reporting outcomes of simulation training and categorized studies based on the Kirkpatrick model of training evaluation. Kirkpatrick level 4 studies reporting patient outcomes were identified and included in this review. Existing evidence is promising demonstrating patient benefits as a result of simulation training for central line placement obstetric emergencies cataract surgery laparoscopic inguinal hernia repair and team training. "Moving the Needle: Simulations Impact on Patient Outcomes."
