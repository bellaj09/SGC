Virtual Reality VR is a new technology consisting on a graphic environment in which the user not only has the feeling of being physically present in a virtual world but he/she can interact with it. The first VR workstations were designed for big companies in order to create environments that simulate certain situations to train professionals. However at this moment a great expansion of this technology is taking place in several fields including the area of health. Especially interesting for us is the use of VR as a therapeutic tool in the treatment of psychological disorders. Compared to the traditional treatments VR has many advantages e.g. it is a protected environment for the patient he/she can re-experience many times the feared situation etc.. There are already data on the effectiveness of this technology in the treatment of different psychological disorders; here anxiety disorders eating disorders and sexual disorders are reviewed. Finally this chapter ends with some words about the limitations of VR and future perspectives. Virtual reality and psychotherapy.
