High-profile cases of medical errors in the USA and UK and major reports from organisations such as the US Institute of Medicine and UK Senate of Surgery have sensitised the public and medical profession. Training is a key area that must be tackled to positively affect the problem of medical errors especially in surgery and interventional cardiology. Despite the radically novel skills required for minimally invasive surgery or interventional cardiology current training has gone largely unchanged. At the end of the 20th century the public and the medical profession have concluded that training on patients is no longer acceptable. Virtual reality training for the operating room and cardiac catheterisation laboratory.
