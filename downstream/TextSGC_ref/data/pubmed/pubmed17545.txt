Visually disabled people increasingly use computers in everyday life thanks to novel assistive technologies better tailored to their cognitive functioning. Like sighted people many are interested in computer games - videogames and audio-games. Tactile-games are beginning to emerge. The Tactile Radar is a device through which a visually disabled person is able to detect distal obstacles. In this study it is connected to a computer running a tactile-game. The game consists in finding and collecting randomly arranged coins in a virtual room. Tactile Radar: experimenting a computer game with visually disabled.
