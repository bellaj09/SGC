It is essential to minimize pain and distress during painful procedures in children. This study examined the effect of using non-immersive Virtual Reality VR during a needle-related procedure on reported pain or distress of children and adolescents in a paediatric oncology unit and surveyed their response to the use of VR-equipment during the procedure. The use of Virtual Reality for needle-related procedural pain and distress in children and adolescents in a paediatric oncology unit.
