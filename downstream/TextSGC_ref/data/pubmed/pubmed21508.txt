Recent innovations in posttraumatic stress disorder PTSD research have identified new treatments with significant potential as well as novel enhancements to empirically-validated treatments. This paper reviews emerging psychotherapeutic and pharmacologic interventions for the treatment of PTSD. It examines the evidence for a range of interventions from social and family-based treatments to technological-based treatments. It describes recent findings regarding novel pharmacologic approaches including propranolol ketamine prazosin and methylenedioxymethamphetamine. Special emphasis is given to the description of virtual reality and D-cycloserine as enhancements to prolonged exposure therapy. Emerging treatments for PTSD.
