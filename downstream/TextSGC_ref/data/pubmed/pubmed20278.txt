Training the new generation of urologic oncologist from a surgical perspective poses unique challenges. The advent of minimally invasive surgical procedures coupled with the need to perform open surgical procedures has significantly increased the demands upon both the trainer and trainee. The learning and practice of complex procedures demand continuous improvements in surgical training programs. This review discusses some theoretical and practical issues to be considered for the successful and safe transmission of surgical skills in an era of increasing regulations. Training the urologic oncologist of the future: where are the challenges?
