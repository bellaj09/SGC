The massification of higher education is often associated with poor student engagement poor development of their critical thinking inadequate feedback and poor student throughput. These factors necessitate the need to devise novel innovative methods to teach assess and provide feedback to learners to counter the restrictions imposed due to the large class learning environments. This study was conducted to ascertain the perceptions of 1st year medical students and staff at the Nelson Mandela School of Medicine regarding the value of poster presentations as a strategy to enhance learning assessment and feedback. Using poster presentation to assess large classes: a case study of a first-year undergraduate module at a South African university.
