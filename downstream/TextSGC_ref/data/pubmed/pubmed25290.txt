Active performance is crucial for motor learning and together with motivation is believed to be associated with a better rehabilitation outcome. Virtual reality VR is an innovative approach to engage and motivate patients during training. There is promising evidence for its efficiency in retraining upper limb function. However there is insufficient proof for its effectiveness in gait training. Virtual reality to augment robot-assisted gait training in non-ambulatory patients with a subacute stroke: a pilot randomized controlled trial.
