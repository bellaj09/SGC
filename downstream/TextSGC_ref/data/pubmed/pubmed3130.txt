Severe stress in social situations is a core symptom of social anxiety disorder SAD. Connectivity between the amygdala and cortical regions is thought to be important for emotion regulation a function that is compromised in SAD. However it has never been tested if and how this connectivity pattern changes under conditions of stress-inducing social evaluative threat. Here we investigate changes in cortical-amygdala coupling in SAD during the anticipation of giving a public speech. Altered cortical-amygdala coupling in social anxiety disorder during the anticipation of giving a public speech.
