Virtual augmented and mixed reality environments are increasingly being developed and used to address functional rehabilitation goals related to physical cognitive social and psychological impairments. For example a child with an acquired brain injury may participate in virtual rehabilitation to address impairments in balance attention turn taking and engagement in therapy. The trend toward virtual rehabilitation first gained momentum with the adoption of commercial off-the-shelf active video gaming consoles e.g. Nintendo Wii and XBox. Now we are seeing the rapid emergence of customized rehabilitation-specific systems that integrate technological advances in virtual reality visual effects motion tracking physiological monitoring and robotics.
