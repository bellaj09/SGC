"Many adolescents in special education are affected by anxiety in addition to their behavioral problems. Anxiety leads to substantial long-term problems and may underlie disruptive behaviors in the classroom as a result of the individuals inability to tolerate anxiety-provoking situations. Thus interventions in special needs schools that help adolescents cope with anxiety and in turn diminish disruptive classroom behaviors are needed." Efficacy of a Virtual Reality Biofeedback Game DEEP to Reduce Anxiety and Disruptive Classroom Behavior: Single-Case Study.
