This paper highlights aspects related to current research and thinking about ethical issues in relation to Brain Computer Interface BCI and Brain-Neuronal Computer Interfaces BNCI research through the experience of one particular project BrainAble which is exploring and developing the potential of these technologies to enable people with complex disabilities to control computers. It describes how ethical practice has been developed both within the multidisciplinary research team and with participants. BNCI systems as a potential assistive technology: ethical issues and participatory research in the BrainAble project.
