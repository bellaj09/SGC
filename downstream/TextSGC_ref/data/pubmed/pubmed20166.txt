New technologies can profoundly change the way we understand psychiatric pathologies and addictive disorders. New concepts are emerging with the development of more accurate means of collecting live data computerized questionnaires and the use of passive data. Digital phenotyping a paradigmatic example refers to the use of computerized measurement tools to capture the characteristics of different psychiatric disorders. Similarly machine learning-a form of artificial intelligence-can improve the classification of patients based on patterns that clinicians have not always considered in the past. Remote or automated interventions web-based or smartphone-based apps as well as virtual reality and neurofeedback are already available or under development. e-Addictology: An Overview of New Technologies for Assessing and Intervening in Addictive Behaviors.
