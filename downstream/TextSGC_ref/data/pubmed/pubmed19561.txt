Behavioral achievements are the product of brain maturation. During postnatal development the medial temporal lobe completes its maturation and children acquire new memory abilities. In recent years virtual reality-based tasks have been introduced in the neuropsychology field to assess different cognitive functions. In this work desktop virtual reality tasks are combined with classic psychometric tests to assess spatial abilities in 4- to 10-year-old children. Developmental gender differences in children in a virtual spatial memory task.
