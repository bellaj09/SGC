The proposed implementation of work hour restrictions has presented a significant challenge of maintaining the quality of resident education and ensuring adequate hands-on experience that is essential for novice surgeons. To maintain the level of resident surgical competency revision of the apprentice model of surgical education to include supplementary educational methods such as laboratory and virtual reality VR simulations have become frequent topics of discussion. We aimed to better understand the role of supplementary educational methods in Canadian neurosurgery residency training. Supplementary Educational Models in Canadian Neurosurgery Residency Programs.
