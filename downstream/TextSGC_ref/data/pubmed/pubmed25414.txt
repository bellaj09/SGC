Training competent and professional surgeons efficiently and effectively requires innovation and modernization of educational methods. Today\s medical learner is quite adept at using multiple platforms to gain information providing surgical educators with numerous innovative avenues to promote learning. With the growth of technology and the restriction of work hours in surgical education there has been an increase in use of simulation including virtual reality robotics telemedicine and gaming. The use of simulation has shifted the learning of basic surgical skills to the laboratory reserving limited time in the operating room for the acquisition of complex surgical skills".
