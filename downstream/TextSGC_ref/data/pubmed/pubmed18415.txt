Face-to-face and text-based social support have been shown to attenuate stress responses in past studies. Yet as social interactions increasingly take place online by means of virtual humans our objective was to examine whether different forms of social support virtual vs. real prior to the Trier Social Stress Test TSST would also decrease stress. Additionally the effect on subsequent real-life social behaviors helping and approach was evaluated while controlling for affective states and social presence. Virtual social support buffers stress response: An experimental comparison of real-life and virtual support prior to a social stressor.
