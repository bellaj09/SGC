Cerebral Palsy CP is characterised by variable difficulties in muscular action resulting in inability of the individual to perform functional movement. An option to provide functionality to the individual with CP is the use of computer innovation. The aim of this paper was to verify if there was any performance improvement in a task performed in a virtual environment and if there was transfer to the task performed in the real environment and vice versa in this population. The use of a task through virtual reality in cerebral palsy using two different interaction devices concrete and abstract - a cross-sectional randomized study.
