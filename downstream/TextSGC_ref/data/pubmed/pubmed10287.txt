Body composition is increasingly being recognized as an important prognostic factor for health outcomes across cancer liver cirrhosis and critically ill patients. Computed tomography CT scans when taken as part of routine care provide an excellent opportunity to precisely measure the quantity and quality of skeletal muscle and adipose tissue. However manual analysis of CT scans is costly and time-intensive limiting the widespread adoption of CT-based measurements of body composition.
