Pedestrian injuries are a leading cause of paediatric injury. Effective practical and cost-efficient behavioural interventions to teach young children street crossing skills are needed. They must be empirically supported and theoretically based. Virtual reality VR offers promise to fill this need and teach child pedestrian safety skills for several reasons including: A repeated unsupervised practice without risk of injury B automated feedback on crossing success or failure C tailoring to child skill levels: D appealing and fun training environment and E most recently given technological advances potential for broad dissemination using mobile smartphone technology. Virtual reality by mobile smartphone: improving child pedestrian safety.
