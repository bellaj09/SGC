A key symptom of posttraumatic stress disorder PTSD is hyperreactivity to trauma-relevant stimuli. Though physiological arousal is reliably elevated in PTSD the question remains whether this arousal responds to treatment. Virtual reality VR has been posited to increase emotional engagement during prolonged exposure therapy PE for PTSD by augmenting imaginal exposures with trauma-relevant sensory information. However the comparative effects of VR exposure therapy VRE have received limited empirical inquiry. Changes in physiological reactivity in response to the trauma memory during prolonged exposure and virtual reality exposure therapy for posttraumatic stress disorder.
