To investigate the feasibility of brain-computer interface BCI with patients on an inpatient spinal cord injury SCI unit. Research Method/Design: This study included 25 participants aged 18-64 who sustained traumatic or nontraumatic SCI and did not have severe cognitive or psychiatric impairment. Participants completed a variety of screening measures related to cognition psychological disposition pain and technology experience/interest. The Emotiv electroencephalography system was used in conjunction with a cube rotation and manipulation game presented on a laptop computer. Brain-computer interface for individuals after spinal cord injury.
