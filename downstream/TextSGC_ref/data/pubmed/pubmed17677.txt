"Since the first deep brain stimulation DBS performed for movement disorder more than a decade ago DBS has become a standard operation for advanced Parkinsons disease. Its indications are expanding to areas of dystonia psychiatric conditions and refractory epilepsy. Additionally a new set of DBS-related complications have arisen. Many teams found a slow learning curve from this complication-prone operation. We would like to investigate complications arising from 100 DBS electrode insertions and its prevention." Complications of deep brain stimulation: a collective review.
