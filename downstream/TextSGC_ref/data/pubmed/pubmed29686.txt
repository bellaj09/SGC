This paper presents an applied concept of a brain-computer interface BCI student research laboratory BCI-LAB at the Life Science Center of TARA University of Tsukuba Japan. Several successful case studies of the student projects are reviewed together with the BCI Research Award 2014 winner case. The BCI-LAB design and project-based teaching philosophy is also explained. Future teaching and research directions summarize the review. Student teaching and research laboratory focusing on brain-computer interface paradigms--A creative environment for computer science students.
